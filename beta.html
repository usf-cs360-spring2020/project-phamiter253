After finishing an anime, I want to find another great show or movie and usually look on MyAnimeList. On the website, I can find all the information about any anime, compare ratings and rankings, look for similar genres, or read reviews. There are so many ways to find something new but I wonder....

Which year premiered the most high ranked and most popular animes?

Introduction

Understanding the Data & Encodings

With the three csv files in the dataset I filtered out less relevant columns, animes that premiered earlier than 1986, any anime that was categorized as Hentai, and rows with blank cells.

Ok. To understand the graph below, you should know:

  • Black nodes connected to the center node represent a year in between 1986 & 2019
  • Different colored nodes/leaves represent an anime
  • A black border around a leaf node means that the anime is ranked in the
    Top 100 because of Overall Scores or Top 100 in Popularity (highest rated & most members) on MyAnimeList
  • A red border around a leaf node means that the anime is ranked in the
    Top 100 on MyAnimeList because of Overall Scores & Top 100 in Popularity (highest rated & most members) on MyAnimeList

How to Use this Graph

Understanding the Force.... Directed Tree


This graph visualizes animes listed in the anime database, MyAnimeList.net.
Here are some features of the graph:

Selection Dropdown

The 'Select Year Range' Dropdown has 7 options. The selected option changes the data input and filters out other year choices.

Color Changing Radio Buttons

The 'Ratings', 'Gender', & 'Age Groups' Radio Buttons display the color encodings based on the checked category.

Tooltip Details & Chart

The tooltips displays the year and number of animes released during that year and number the red and black bordered nodes, if the mouse is over a black node on the graph. If the mouse is over a leaf, the tooltip displays the name, score, rank, and (not completely implemented yet)overall stats of the selected anime.

Individual Anime Information & Stats

Clicking on a leaf node displays ratings, genres, synopsis, and the data for the selected anime.

Genre Brushing

Double clicking on a leaf will focus on other leaves that share the same first genre.

Conclusions

  • The year 2011 has the most favorites.
